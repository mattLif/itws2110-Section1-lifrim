From the Constitution of the United States

Home Preamble Articles Amendments

Amendment III

"No Soldier shall, in time of peace be quartered in any house, without the consent of the Owner; nor in time of war, but in a manner to be prescribed by law."

Analysis

The Third Amendment to the U.S. Constitution says that soldiers cannot be housed in private homes without the owner's consent. This amendment was originally written as a result of when British soldiers would forcibly quarter themselves within colonists' homes. While the literal contents of this amendment are not very relevant today, the idea that it reinforces is. A person's home and personal privacy shall not be breached by the government, unless there is lawful reason to do so.
