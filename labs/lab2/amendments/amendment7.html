Amendment VII

"In Suits at common law, where the value in controversy shall exceed twenty dollars, the right of trial by jury shall be preserved, and no fact tried by a jury shall be otherwise reexamined in any Court of the United States, than according to the rules of common law."

Analysis

This amendment’s purpose is to grant citizens the right to a trial by a jury of one’s peers in federal civil cases involving disputes with money or property worth more than 20$. This amendment also prohibits federal courts from reexamining facts that have already been decided by a jury, ensuring the finality of a jury’s judgement. It also says that issues of law are determined by a judge and issues of fact are decided by a jury.
